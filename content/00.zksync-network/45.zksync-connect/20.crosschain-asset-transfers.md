## What are crosschain asset transfers?

Crosschain asset transfers mean that you can send any asset directly from one ZKsync chain to another ZKsync chain without using a bridge.
They will be available on mainnet chains that settle on ZKsync Gateway after the `v31` upgrade.

## How do they work?

With ZKsync Connect crosschain asset transfers,
assets are natively tracked across ZKsync chains.
When transferred, the asset is fully burned on Chain A before getting minted on Chain B.
This differs from traditional bridging, which instead locks the asset in a contract.

Assets are tracked on each L2 chain through asset tracker contracts, which are deployed on the L1, Gateway, and all L2 chains.
The asset transfer contracts ensure that no chain is able to transfer more assets than it has.

The assets never leave the ZKsync ecosystem, and never touch the L1.

Assets can be transferred via transaction bundles through the  `InteropHandler` contract.
Before transferring, the asset must first be registered with the asset tracker contract.
This registration only needs to happen once.

## What can I transfer?

You can transfer any asset that is registered with the asset tracker contract.

## Can I transfer an asset without a contract?

For the first version of interop, the `InteropHandler` contract requires that the recipient is a contract that implements `IERC7786Recipient` interface.
This means that a contract is required to facilitate all asset transfers, including for ETH.
EOA wallets cannot directly receive asset transfers without a contract to help facilitate the transfer.
In a future upgrade, direct transfers will be enabled.
